# Environmental Data Terms of Use

The environmental measurement values displayed by this project are provided by the Technology Club
of Thrace Environmental Station.

Official source:

- https://steth.gr/env

These terms apply to the use, display, redistribution and presentation of the station measurements,
including values obtained through this project's API endpoints.

## 1. Attribution Required

Any public display or redistribution of the measurements must clearly identify the Technology Club
of Thrace Environmental Station as the data source.

At minimum, public displays must include:

- the Technology Club of Thrace logo, unmodified;
- a visible source label, such as "Measurement Source";
- a link or reference to https://steth.gr/env.

The attribution must be visible and understandable to a normal viewer.

## 2. No Misrepresentation

You may not present the measurements as your own data, as data from another station, or as data
produced by a different organization.

If you modify the software, change the design or add your own organization name, the original
measurement source must remain clear.

## 3. Respectful Informational Use

The measurements may be used for educational, informational and public-interest presentations.

Suitable examples include:

- school displays;
- classroom and STEM activities;
- local community information displays;
- waiting room displays;
- office or clinic screens that show environmental information to visitors.

The measurements must not be used as an unrelated advertising surface, as a misleading promotional
claim or as the basis for commercial resale.

## 4. No Safety-Critical Reliance

The measurements are provided for information and education.
They must not be used as the sole basis for medical, emergency, safety-critical, legal, regulatory
or industrial decisions.

## 5. Data Integrity

When measurements are shown publicly, they must be displayed in a way that preserves their meaning.

You may not intentionally alter the values, timestamps, units or labels in a way that misleads
viewers.

If values are transformed, summarized or interpreted, the display should clearly indicate that an
interpretation or derived presentation is being shown.

## 6. API Use

Applications and devices that consume the project's API should respect the server-side cache and
avoid unnecessary request rates.

Recommended request interval:

- no more than one request per minute for normal displays;
- longer intervals are preferred for devices that do not need live updates.

Do not bypass the project cache in order to put unnecessary load on the original ThingsBoard server.

## 7. No Warranty

The measurements are provided "as is".
The data provider and project maintainers do not guarantee accuracy, completeness, availability or
continuous operation.

Use the data responsibly and with appropriate context.
